Partner Introductions

Our project is made possible through collaboration with the following key partners:

MotionInput Games

MotionInput Games is a company focused on developing touchless interaction technologies that allow users to control digital systems through gestures and motion-based inputs. They are supporting this project by providing mentorship and guidance throughout the development process, particularly in refining requirements. MotionInput Games also plans to publish and showcase our work, giving the project practical relevance and visibility beyond the academic environment. Their continuous feedback and expertise in motion-based interaction systems have helped shape our design decisions and ensure that our solutions remain innovative, accessible, and implementable in real-world settings.

National Autistic Society (NAS)

The National Autistic Society (NAS) is a UK-based organisation that supports autistic individuals and their families through specialist schools, training, and educational programmes. NAS acts as a key partner in this project by providing educational expertise and real-world insight into how interactive technologies can be used in classroom environments. Their schools will be using the developed technologies as part of their teaching and learning activities, ensuring that the solutions are practical and beneficial for students. Meetings and feedback sessions with NAS representatives have played an important role in shaping the product, helping the team understand user needs, accessibility requirements, and the importance of designing inclusive and easy-to-use systems for educational contexts.

Intel

Intel is a global technology company that provides advanced computing solutions and technical support for innovative projects. The project makes use of Intel’s technologies and computing infrastructure, and the final solutions are intended to run on Intel-based machines, ensuring reliable performance and scalability. Their support has enabled the team to experiment with advanced features and improve system performance while ensuring that the final product remains efficient and accessible for real-world deployment.
