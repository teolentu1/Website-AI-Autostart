AI-Autostart

Enabling Inclusive Interaction Across Kiosks and Games Through Adaptive Sensing

Project Abstract

Problem Statement

Many everyday interactive systems, such as self-service kiosks and digital games, are not designed with accessibility as a default. Users with visual impairments, limited mobility, or neurodivergent needs are often required to manually enable accessibility settings or rely on external assistive devices, creating barriers to independent and comfortable interaction. In busy public environments, these limitations can lead to exclusion, frustration, and reduced usability for disabled users.

Your Solution

AI-Autostart addresses this problem by introducing a hands-free, adaptive accessibility platform that automatically initiates and configures interactions based on user presence and intent. Running as a lightweight system tray application, the system combines computer vision and Wi-Fi proximity sensing to detect when a user approaches and signals interaction readiness, such as through sustained hand-raising gestures. Based on this detection, AI-Autostart seamlessly activates either an adaptive self-service kiosk interface or a fully audio-based, no-UI game for blind and visually impaired users, without requiring physical contact or manual setup.

Achievement and Impact

The project demonstrates how accessibility can be embedded as an automatic system behaviour rather than an optional feature. By operating entirely locally and prioritising low resource usage, AI-Autostart preserves user privacy while remaining deployable on standard hardware. The resulting prototypes highlight the potential for more inclusive public and personal digital systems, promoting independence, reducing social discomfort, and contributing to a more equitable interaction experience for users with diverse accessibility needs.
